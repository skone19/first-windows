
1. Converting Between Inline and Display Modes
Objective: Compare the display of the same formulas in inline and display modes.

\documentclass{article}
\usepackage[T1]{fontenc}
\usepackage{amsmath}

\begin{document}

\section*{Inline vs Display Mode}

Inline mode: $E = mc^2$, $y = ax^2 + bx + c$, $\sum_{i=1}^n i = \frac{n(n+1)}{2}$

Same formulas in display mode:
\[
E = mc^2
\]
\[
y = ax^2 + bx + c
\]
\[
\sum_{i=1}^n i = \frac{n(n+1)}{2}
\]

\end{document}

 Additional Exercises for Deepening Understanding
5. Advanced amsmath Usage
latex
\documentclass{article}
\usepackage[T1]{fontenc}
\usepackage{amsmath}

\begin{document}

\section*{Advanced amsmath Environments}

Multiple alignment:
\begin{align*}
x &= y + z & a &= b + c \\
x^2 &= y^2 + 2yz + z^2 & a^2 &= b^2 + 2bc + c^2
\end{align*}

Equation gathering:
\begin{gather*}
e^{i\theta} = \cos\theta + i\sin\theta \\
\cos^2\theta + \sin^2\theta = 1 \\
\tan\theta = \frac{\sin\theta}{\cos\theta}
\end{gather*}

Multiline equation:
\begin{multline*}
p(x) = 3x^6 + 14x^5y + 590x^4y^2 + 19x^3y^3 \\ 
- 12x^2y^4 - 12xy^5 + 2y^6 - a^3b^3
\end{multline*}

\end{document}
6. Bold Mathematics with bm
latex
\documentclass{article}
\usepackage[T1]{fontenc}
\usepackage{amsmath}
\usepackage{bm}

\begin{document}

\section*{Bold Mathematics}

With \verb|\boldmath|:
{\boldmath
\[
\nabla \times \mathbf{E} = -\frac{\partial \mathbf{B}}{\partial t}
\]
}

With \verb|\bm|:
\[
\bm{\nabla} \times \bm{E} = -\frac{\partial \bm{B}}{\partial t}
\]

Mixed bold and normal:
\[
\bm{F} = q(\bm{E} + \bm{v} \times \bm{B})
\]

\end{document}
